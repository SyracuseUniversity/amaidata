**Magazine, News and Digital Journalism**

_S.I. Newhouse School of Public Communications_

instructor or instructors and the department.

_S.I. Newhouse School of Public Communications_  
0 credit(s) Every semester

student must receive a grade of P.  
PREREQ MND 305  

  * ### MND 499 - Thesis

_S.I. Newhouse School of Public Communications_

_S.I. Newhouse School of Public Communications_  
1 credit(s) Every semester

* * *

direction of TheNewsHouse.com while learning and experimenting with the
technical, visual and social media aspects of producing digital content across
multiple platforms.  
PREREQ MND 505

_S.I. Newhouse School of Public Communications_  
3 credit(s) At least 1x fall or spring

PREREQ MND 408

_S.I. Newhouse School of Public Communications_

_S.I. Newhouse School of Public Communications_

_S.I. Newhouse School of Public Communications_

PREREQ MND 305

_S.I. Newhouse School of Public Communications_

Double Numbered with MMI 665

front-end development frameworks, and web design to deliver mobile-friendly
web pages. Students will research and evaluate an audience to perform a
website redesign. Additional work required for graduate students.

  * ### MMI 425 - Advanced Media Business

_S.I. Newhouse School of Public Communications_  
3 credit(s) At least 1x fall or spring

* * *

Double Numbered with MMI 625  
How tech-empowered consumers and data are reshaping the roles of big brands,
big tech, and big media, including: TV, streaming, film, audio, gaming, print,
online, web3, NFTs, blockchain, the metaverse, AR/MR/VR, and beyond.

  * ### MMI 427 - New Media Venture Launch

_S.I. Newhouse School of Public Communications_  
3 credit(s) At least 1x fall or spring

* * *

Double Numbered with MMI 627  
Students will learn the process of preparing and validating new media business
ideas for launching - whether freelance, small business or high-scale
ventures. In the process, all learn tools and techniques required to become
media innovators and entrepreneurs. Additional work required of graduate
students.  
Shared Competencies Information Literacy and Technical Agility; Critical and
Creative Thinking

_S.I. Newhouse School of Public Communications_

_S.I. Newhouse School of Public Communications_

* * *

  * ### SMC 330 - Topics in Esports and Media

_S.I. Newhouse School of Public Communications_

_S.I. Newhouse School of Public Communications_  
3 credit(s) At least 1x fall or spring

* * *

The ethical challenges facing the sports communications industry are vast and
far-reaching. Explored through traditional ethical theory, this course
evaluates and analyzes the decision-making process unique to sports
